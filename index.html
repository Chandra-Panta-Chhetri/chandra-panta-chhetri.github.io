Welcome to Team Work

A skillful group of CSCC01 students building an MVP that will replace togotoronto.com and showcase local restaurants and restaurant owners in Scarborough.

The Problem

  • Scarborough food can only say so much alone. Chefs and owners have story to tell, but who is listening?
  • Downtown and uptown Toronto dining get all the attention, including the togotoronto.com website
  • Scarborough food is often lower margin and paying delivery services 30% is not sustainable, especially during the pandemic
Goals
  • Create an outstanding platform where restaurant owners and chefs of Scarborough can tell their story and inspire people to try their food
  • Promote local restaurants in Scarborough and improve the engagement and number of patrons accessing local food.
  • Hone our development skills while successfully follow current best software engineering practices
Strengths
  • As our team name suggests, we have excellent teamwork skills developed from working on past school projects together
  • We have exceptional skills in web development developed from past coop jobs

Project Requirements
  • Search engine allowing searches by type of cuisine, location, affordability, ratings and other categories
  • Embed video clips of chefs discussing their menus and providing cooking tips and stories of how recipes were developed, where and how certain ingredients are produced and other interesting food stories
  • Easy to navigate for patrons, and easy to access food (ordering, transaction engine) and to easily connect this activity to the restaurants themselves
  • Allow for the creation of digital social networks, building promotional capacity, and a means to connect with the community of restaurants and the people that frequent them
  • promotions and community element may be gamified to increase engagement with target audiences
  • Analytics engine providing insights on consumer behavior
  • Ensure the platform is easy to use so non-technologically sophisticated audiences can update content
  • All software developed would be open source, provided as part of a community initiative
